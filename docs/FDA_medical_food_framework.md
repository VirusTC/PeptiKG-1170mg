## 1. The Legal Ordering Framework

While **PeptiKG-1170mg** is not an FDA-approved pharmaceutical drug requiring a traditional drugstore prescription, it is legally structured to be administered **under the active supervision of a licensed physician** as a Medical Food.

* **Distinctive Nutritional Requirement:** Under federal law, a physician can order or direct a medical food when a patient displays a documented metabolic or physiological state that creates distinct nutritional needs that cannot be managed by modifying a standard diet.
* **The Non-Disease Paradigm:** The clinical target is not a disease; it is an underlying physiological state: **Protein-Energy Malnutrition, Hypercatabolic Nitrogen Wasting, or Intestinal Absorptive Failure**.

### Pharmacological Rationale
Intracellular structural matrices, viral vesicle envelopes, and parasitic organisms leverage host amino acid pools to stabilize their own replication complexes and construct protective cellular walls. Introducing high-potency protein repletion to an unmedicated patient provides an immediate metabolic resource that may inadvertently accelerate the proliferation rate of the pathogen.

### Clinical Directive
1. **Withhold Repletion:** Do not introduce PeptiKG-1170mg during acute, unmedicated phases of viral infections, oncological tumor progression, or parasitic infestations.
2. **Pharmaceutical Clearance:** Establish the patient on targeted antiviral protocols, oncology chemotherapy/targeted therapy, or antiparasitic medications for a minimum of **three consecutive weeks**.
3. **Repletion Phase:** Once laboratory markers confirm that the target pathogen replication baseline is successfully suppressed or controlled, initialize PeptiKG-1170mg to rebuild damaged host tissues, support skin boundary closure, and correct systemic protein deficits safely.
